About Whitlock Avenue

Whitlock Avenue is one of Milton's most distinctive corridors, and it's not really one street. It's three. At the Cobban end sits a full-amenity condominium tower that brings a metropolitan lifestyle to Milton's growing southeast. Move further along and the avenue opens into Mattamy-built townhome rows. At the Ford end, you find newer detached pockets with established residential character. Three different buying experiences share the same address, and the Whitlock you end up in depends entirely on where you land on it.

Our analysis of this avenue covers 244 transactions in recent market activity. That makes Whitlock one of Milton's most liquid addresses. The data and the lived experience of working with buyers and sellers here complement each other, and both are worth considering.

The homes on Whitlock

The condominium tower is what defines the street. Unit sizes run from 1-bedroom layouts under 700 square feet through 3-bedroom units approaching 1,400 square feet, with 9-foot ceilings as the base spec. Most units we've shown here come with quartz countertops, stainless appliance packages, and upgraded cabinetry. Interior finishes consistently reflect the upper-mid tier of Milton's condo market.

Upper-floor units face west and look out toward the Niagara Escarpment. That view alone commands a real premium inside the building. Top-floor units turn over faster than middle floors when they list. If view matters to you, know that it will affect what you pay and how quickly the unit moves.

The townhome section is also Mattamy-built, predominantly 3-storey 3-bedroom units on approximately 21-foot frontages with built-in garages. Finishes hold to the same modern standard as the condo tower. At the Ford end, you find newer detached homes on 30 to 45 foot lots, 4 to 5 bedroom layouts between 2,000 and 3,000 square feet, typically trading in the $1.2 million to $1.5 million range.

Living here: amenities and lifestyle

The Whitlock condominium building offers one of Milton's most comprehensive amenity packages. A full concierge service, fitness centre, yoga studio, rooftop terrace with BBQ facilities, co-working space, media room, party room, and dedicated pet amenities. For buyers who value on-site amenities, this building is a strong option in the local market.

That matters for a specific buyer. If you're moving from Toronto or Mississauga and want to keep urban-grade amenities in your new home, this is one of the few buildings in Milton that can deliver that experience. When evaluating a unit, we always walk clients through the condo fee structure so you understand exactly what's included, and can weigh it against how you'll actually use each amenity.

The townhome and detached sections of the avenue offer a different daily rhythm. Private driveways, garage access, backyards, and the suburban character many families prefer. Two styles of living on the same address. Each has its own appeal, and knowing which one matches your preferences upfront makes the search far more efficient.

Market behaviour

244 transactions, 21 sales, 223 leases. That mix tells you something important about the condominium tower: it's one of Milton's most active rental markets. A good portion of units are held as long-term investments, which makes this one of the more active rental corridors in town. For anyone considering the street, that's useful context whether you're buying to live in, or buying as an investment.

The data for renters and investors is clean. Two-bedroom condos typically lease at $2,150 per month and find tenants within 37 days. Proven rental demand with real numbers behind it.

Townhome pricing on Whitlock has evolved over recent quarters. In early 2025, comparable units were trading around the $1.04 million typical level. By late 2025, the same product was clearing in the $748,000 to $875,000 range. Meanwhile, condo rents held flat through the same period. Each shift creates specific considerations for owners thinking about timing and buyers thinking about positioning, and our team can walk through what it means for your situation.

Across all product types on the avenue, the typical sold price sits at $986,000, with sales ranging from $748,000 for townhomes up to $1.47 million for larger detached homes. Average time to sell is 72 days. That's a measured pace, which suggests careful consideration on both sides of transactions.

Getting around

Milton GO Station is the defining commute asset for this avenue. Most of the buyers we've worked with on Whitlock chose the street specifically for GO proximity. The station is roughly 12 minutes by car, with the Milton Line running direct to Union Station in Toronto. For buyers commuting to downtown Toronto, Whitlock is one of the stronger addresses in Milton for that use case.

Highway access is similarly strong. The 401 is 7 minutes via the James Snow or Bronte exits. The 407 is 5 minutes (useful for toll-friendly trips to Toronto or Pearson). The QEW is 18 minutes if you're heading toward Hamilton. Milton Transit Route 5 stops at Whitlock and Bronte, a 200-metre walk from most of the avenue. For airport runs, Pearson is 28 minutes via the 407.

Schools and families

Because Whitlock runs through two neighbourhoods, school catchments split along the avenue. Families considering this street should verify the catchment against their specific address. This isn't a detail to assume. High school assignments across the avenue include Milton District Secondary, Craig Kielburger Secondary, and Bishop Reding (Catholic). On the elementary side, Boyne Public School is walking distance from the southern end, with other assignments depending on exact location.

For families with young children or school-age kids, we always recommend confirming the specific catchment before offering. The Halton District School Board boundary tool is the source of truth, and we walk clients through it when a decision is near.

Every street in Milton offers something different. If your priorities line up more with one of these, they're worth a serious look:

  • Want larger lots and more detached inventory? The detached portion of Whitlock is a smaller share of the avenue's supply. For detached-dominant streets with wider lots, explore Bowes, Willmott, and Scott.
  • Want a quieter cul-de-sac feel rather than a through-street? Milton has many court-style streets with lower traffic and tighter community feel. Explore court-style streets across Milton.

The detached and semi-detached homes on Whitlock are concentrated at the Ford end of the street. Newer Mattamy builds on 30 to 45 foot lots, typically 4 to 5 bedrooms, 2,000 to 3,000 square feet. These are the family-sized addresses on the avenue, and they trade very differently from the condo tower. Semi-detached supply here is minimal. Only a handful have transacted, but they offer a solid entry point for buyers who want the freehold lifestyle at a lower ticket than a fully detached home.

10 — Common questions

What people ask about Whitlock Avenue.

Who built the homes on Whitlock Avenue?
The majority of homes on Whitlock Avenue were built by Mattamy Homes. Identified in 102 of 244 listing descriptions. A small number (2 listings) were built by Great Gulf. The Mattamy builds are concentrated in the Cobban neighbourhood, part of their broader Milton development.
What is the average home price on Whitlock Avenue?
The typical sold price on Whitlock Avenue is $986,000. Sales have ranged from $748,000 for a two-bedroom townhome to $1,465,000 for a five-bedroom detached home.
How many homes sold on Whitlock Avenue in the last 2 years?
Whitlock Avenue has recorded 21 sales and 223 leases in recent market activity. The street is dominated by condominium rental activity, 185 of the transactions were condo apartment leases.
What is the typical home size on Whitlock Avenue?
Whitlock Avenue has a wide range of home sizes. Condo apartments range from one-bedroom layouts under 700 sqft to three-bedroom layouts approaching 1,400 sqft. Townhomes are predominantly 3-storey, 3-bedroom units between 1,100 and 2,000 sqft. Detached homes are 4 to 5 bedroom, typically 2,000 to 3,000 sqft on 30 to 45 foot lots.
What is the typical rent for a condo on Whitlock Avenue?
One-bedroom condos on Whitlock Avenue typically rent for $1,950 per month. Two-bedroom condos typically rent for $2,150 per month. Three-bedroom condos typically rent for $2,800 per month. Rents have held steady across recent quarters.
What neighbourhood is Whitlock Avenue in?
Whitlock Avenue runs through two Milton neighbourhoods. The majority of the street sits in Cobban (220 of 244 records, roughly 90%). The newer, denser end with the condo spine and Mattamy townhome row. A smaller portion runs through Ford (15 records, 6%), which has lower density and more detached homes. Small sections also touch Bowes and Willmott at the edges. If you're looking at a specific address, the neighbourhood will depend on where on the avenue the home sits.
How long do homes on Whitlock Avenue typically take to sell?
Homes on Whitlock Avenue take an average of 72 days to sell (For Sale). Leases move much faster. Typically 37 days on market. Both typically close at 98 to 99 percent of asking price.
Is Whitlock Avenue a good street for investors?
Whitlock Avenue has a strong investor profile due to its high condo density and consistent rental demand. Recent activity shows 223 lease transactions. Approximately 10 times the sales volume. Two-bedroom condos at a typical $2,150 rent against purchase prices in the high $500K to low $700K range produce competitive gross yields for the Milton market.
